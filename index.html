Barbie (2023) Review

June 24, 2023 by Nicholas Smith

Since the announcement of Margot Robbie and Ryan Gosling as Barbie and Ken, Barbie (2023) immediately became one of my most anticipated movies. My excitement continuously grew to the point where I was slightly worried that I'd hyped up a film that could never meet my ridiculously high expectations. Today I've learnt to never doubt Greta Gerwig even in the slightest bit. She's a truly remarkable film maker. Barbie was everything I could've asked for.

I think I underestimated just how funny Barbie was actually gonna be. This movie was absolutely hilarious, my theatre broke out in laughter all throughout this. The cast really delivered. For the most part everyone got their moment to get a line in that really stuck the landing. Even the unexpected pop culture references don't feel forced or out of place. This charismatic cast really elevated this screenplay to hilarious heights.

Margot Robbie continues to prove she's one of the biggest stars of this generation of actors. A nomination is absolutely certain for her. She was the perfect person to bring this Barbie story to life. I fell more in love with her as an actress and I didn't even think that was possible because of how much I already do. She and Gosling were a great pair to build this movie around. Absolutely loved her scenes with America as well who really felt like the heart of the movie. The technical aspects were very impressive. The movie looks absolutely amazing and really has the doll like feel.

This was a hilarious but also very emotional watch. Navigating this world is tricky, it's uncomfortable , painful but also very beautiful. It's difficult for me to put into words but there's just something about the way Noah Baumbach and Greta Gerwig convey the human experience that just hits me. Through the hardships, boredom, confusion and moments where you're not even quite sure who you are there's still a lot of beauty that comes with being alive and their movies remind of that. When Billie Eillish's 'What was I made for' plays during the emotional climax of the film, I was an absolute wreck. I can't wait to watch this again. Greta Gerwig is 3/3!!!

Oppenheimer (2023) Review

July 29, 2023 by Nicholas Smith

The movie is very well made it just didn't personally resonate with me as much as I would've liked because I'm not really familiar with the subject material. The cinematography is stunning and I absolutely love the score but I left this without any strong feelings toward the actual story. I can totally see Oppenheimer being a top contender for the technical categories rightfully so but I think I'll need to rewatch it at some point to truly get it.
